About Us

Sarrat is a town in the province of Ilocos Norte in the Philippines. It is located in the northern part of the island of Luzon. The town is known for its historical and cultural significance, as well as its natural beauty. Cabayugan was how the people called Sarrat by its first settlers led by the village chief Minagel (Maingel) Bang'at and his wife Sarrah, before the arrival of the Spaniards on the latter part of the 16th century.[5] Sarrat is a compound of the couple's names and is believed to be coined by their son Garo.

Historical Significance Cultural Heritage Natural Beauty Agriculture
Sarrat is historically significant because it is the birthplace of Ferdinand Marcos, the former President and dictator of the Philippines. His childhood home, known as the Marcos Ancestral House, is a popular tourist attraction in the town. The town is rich in cultural heritage and is home to various traditional Filipino customs and practices. Local festivals, religious events, and celebrations are an integral part of Sarrat's cultural fabric. Sarrat is surrounded by scenic landscapes, including lush greenery and picturesque countryside. The town is close to Paoay Lake, a popular destination for nature enthusiasts. The lake is known for its serene ambiance and is a great spot for relaxation and picnics. Agriculture is a significant part of the local economy in Sarrat. The fertile land in the region supports the cultivation of various crops and agricultural activities.
